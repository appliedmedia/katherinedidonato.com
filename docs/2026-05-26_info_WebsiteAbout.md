## Bio

Changing the way one thinks about things can improve your quality of life even when circumstances remain the same. This concept is quite powerful and motivated Katherine's desire to become a psychologist. She pursued specialized training in cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT) to help individuals gain mastery over their unhelpful thoughts. This can help you manage painful emotions and cope better with difficult circumstances.

With over 25 years of experience, Katherine brings a collaborative, goal-oriented approach to every session. She works with adults of all ages, including young adults, working-age adults, and seniors, and has particular expertise in anxiety and panic disorders, OCD, depression, infertility, and pregnancy and postpartum mental health.

All sessions are held virtually, allowing Katherine to work with clients throughout New Jersey and New York.

## Therapeutic Philosophy

Unhelpful thoughts are not facts. When you learn to recognize and challenge them, you gain real control over how you feel and how you move through the world. Katherine's work centers on building that skill together with her clients, using evidence-based tools that have been shown to work and that clients can carry with them long after therapy ends.
