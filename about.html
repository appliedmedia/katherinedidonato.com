Hi, I’m Katherine.

Background

Changing the way one thinks about things can improve your quality of life even when circumstances remain the same. This concept is quite powerful, and it motivated Katherine’s desire to become a psychologist.

She pursued specialized training in cognitive behavioral therapy to help individuals gain mastery over their unhelpful thoughts — because when you learn to recognize and challenge them, you gain real control over how you feel and how you move through the world.

Katherine brings a collaborative, goal-oriented approach to every session, using evidence-based tools that have been shown to work and that clients can carry with them long after therapy ends. She has been practicing for over 25 years, beginning in San Francisco in 1998, and has developed particular expertise in anxiety and panic disorders, OCD, depression, infertility, and perinatal mental health.

My approach

Unhelpful thoughts are not facts.

Katherine’s work centers on building that skill together with her clients. Therapy with Katherine is active and structured: you will learn concrete techniques, practice them between sessions, and track real change over time.

She draws primarily from cognitive behavioral therapy, acceptance and commitment therapy, and exposure-based approaches including ERP, adapting each treatment plan to the individual in front of her. The goal is not just to feel better in the moment, but to leave therapy with tools you own.

All sessions are held virtually, allowing Katherine to work with clients throughout New Jersey and New York from wherever is most convenient for you.
