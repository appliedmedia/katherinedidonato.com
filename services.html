Areas of focus

What Katherine treats.

Anxiety & Panic Disorders
Anxiety can feel all-consuming: the racing thoughts, the physical symptoms, the avoidance that slowly narrows your world. Katherine uses cognitive behavioral therapy and exposure-based techniques to help you understand what is driving your anxiety, face it in a manageable way, and reclaim the life you want.
Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD)
OCD responds best to Exposure and Response Prevention (ERP), the gold-standard treatment, which Katherine has specialized training in. ERP works by gradually and systematically confronting the thoughts and situations that trigger obsessions, while resisting compulsive responses, so the cycle loses its grip.
Depression
When depression takes hold, even small tasks can feel impossible. Katherine works collaboratively to identify the patterns in thinking and behavior that maintain low mood, and to build concrete skills for shifting them. Treatment is active and goal-oriented.
Pregnancy, Prenatal & Postpartum
The perinatal period brings profound changes, and sometimes profound struggles: anxiety, depression, birth trauma, or the complicated feelings that come with loss. Katherine supports clients through all stages of this journey with compassion and clinical expertise.
Infertility
Navigating fertility challenges is emotionally exhausting in ways that others can be hard to understand. Katherine provides a space to process grief, fear, uncertainty, and the unique relationship stress that accompanies treatment. Therapy can help you find footing and make decisions from a clearer place.

Treatment approaches

Evidence-based tools that work.

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)

Identifies connections between thoughts, feelings, and behaviors, and builds practical skills to change unhelpful patterns.

Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT)

Develops psychological flexibility by accepting difficult thoughts and feelings, and committing to action guided by personal values.

Exposure and Response Prevention (ERP)

The first-line behavioral treatment for OCD. Gradual, supported exposure to feared situations while resisting compulsive behaviors.

Prolonged Exposure

An evidence-based behavioral approach targeting avoidance and the patterns that maintain anxiety and distress over time.

Format & availability

Virtual sessions, on your schedule.

All sessions are held via telehealth. There are no in-person appointments. This allows Katherine to work with clients throughout New Jersey and New York from wherever is most convenient.

Katherine sees individual adults, including young adults (18–24), adults (25–64), and seniors (65+). Sessions are 45 minutes.

Insurance & fees

Katherine accepts Aetna, Cigna, and many affiliated plans. Out-of-network sessions are $140–$200. A sliding scale is available for clients with financial need.
